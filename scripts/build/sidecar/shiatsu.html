Empathetic touch of shiatsu in Brooklyn

Rooted in traditional Eastern medicine, shiatsu (指圧) is a gentle, hands-on practice from Japan that uses meditative touch and stretching along the energy channels of the body to invite your energy back into natural flow.

Rather than fix or force, each shiatsu session honors your whole being without judgment, opening a space to gently stir the hidden stagnations at the root of dis-ease and help you show up to life’s changes with clarity, resilience, and an open heart.

What to expect in your session

  • Sessions are fully clothed on a comfortable floor mat with cushions provided
  • Wear loose, comfortable clothing for easy movement
  • Each session begins with a gentle assessment of the hara (abdomen) to understand your energetic state
  • Treatment is tailored to your state using stretches and attentive pressure along meridians and specific points
  • The first session includes a relaxed intake chat about your lifestyle, concerns, and goals for treatment
  • Simply relax and listen to your body during the session

Meet your practicioner

A graduate of the Five Lights Center school of shiatsu in New York City, Claire Froelich practiced four years developing an ability to listen and facilitate balance under the guidance of Grandmaster Kumiko Kanayama and Simone Carbonel, in the tradition of Shizuto Masunaga's Zen Shiatsu. She has continued her studies across the U.S. and Italy, including specialized training in ampuku (abdominal massage), and enjoys volunteering shiatsu at community wellness events in New York City.

Claire spent her twenties as a Japanese interpreter in Hokkaido where she experienced the benefits of Eastern medicine first-hand. The island's vivid seasons and abundant wilderness deepened her reverence for the rhythms of nature. While she values language as a stepping stone, she's drawn to the truths words can't reach.
